BUSINESS OVERVIEW

Reliance Industries Limited (RIL) continues to be India's largest private sector company with diversified interests across energy, petrochemicals, retail, telecommunications, and digital services. The company reported consolidated revenues of $104.6 billion for the fiscal year 2023-24, representing a growth of 12.5% over the previous year.

RETAIL BUSINESS

Reliance Retail Limited, a wholly-owned subsidiary of RIL, operates India's largest retail network with over 18,000 stores across the country. The retail business generated revenues of $32.5 billion in FY 2023-24, up from $28.9 billion in the previous year. 

Key acquisitions and ownership structures include:
- Reliance Retail owns 100% of Hamleys, the iconic British toy retailer, acquired in 2019 for approximately $88 million.
- Reliance Retail also owns a majority stake in Netmeds, India's leading online pharmacy platform.
- The company has strategic partnerships with international brands including Marks & Spencer, through a joint venture arrangement.

ENERGY & PETROCHEMICALS

The Oil & Gas division, operated through Reliance Industries Limited, reported revenues of $45.2 billion. The company operates the world's largest refining complex at Jamnagar with a capacity of 1.4 million barrels per day.

RISK FACTORS

The company faces several risk factors that could impact its operations:

1. Market Volatility: Fluctuations in crude oil prices and petrochemical margins could significantly impact profitability. The company's energy business is exposed to global commodity price movements.

2. Regulatory Changes: Changes in government policies, particularly in the telecommunications and retail sectors, could affect business operations. The company operates in highly regulated industries.

3. Competition: Intense competition in retail and telecommunications sectors from both domestic and international players could impact market share and profitability.

4. Currency Fluctuations: The company has significant foreign currency exposure due to international operations and imports. Exchange rate movements could impact financial results.

5. Technology Disruption: Rapid technological changes in the telecommunications and digital services sectors require continuous investment and adaptation.

FINANCIAL HIGHLIGHTS

- Total Assets: $185.3 billion
- Net Debt: $22.7 billion
- EBITDA: $18.5 billion
- Net Profit: $8.2 billion
- Capital Expenditure: $12.4 billion

The company maintains a strong balance sheet with cash and cash equivalents of $15.8 billion as of March 31, 2024.

FUTURE OUTLOOK

Reliance Industries Limited continues to invest in growth areas including renewable energy, with plans to invest $10 billion over the next three years in green energy initiatives. The company is also expanding its retail footprint with plans to add 2,500 new stores in FY 2024-25.
